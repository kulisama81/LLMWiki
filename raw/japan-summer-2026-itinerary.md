Landing in Nagoya on July 1 to spend time with family, then heading to Hakone (onsen), Tokyo, and Kyoto before finishing in Osaka. ## Notes

- **Book Tawaraya Ryokan immediately** — one of Japan's most celebrated ryokan, books out 3–6 months out
- **JR Pass 14-day** (~¥70,000/person; child discount only applies under 12, so both teens pay adult rate) covers: all Shinkansen, Sunrise Express base fare, Kounotori to Kinosaki — buy exchange order before leaving the US. JR Pass is only valid for foreign nationals on Temporary Visitor status; if any family member holds Japanese nationality, they cannot use the pass — calculate individual fares instead
- **Sunrise Express sleeper surcharge**: Nobi Nobi floor berths are free on top of JR Pass; Solo private cabin ~¥6,000–¥7,000/person extra — reserve 1 month in advance, berths sell out fast in summer
- **teamLab Planets**: advance tickets required, buy online before arrival — immersive digital art, recommended for teens
- July heat: plan outdoor sightseeing before 10:00 or after 17:00 — carry cooling towels and electrolytes
- Rainy season (tsuyu) typically ends mid-July; expect some rain and humidity in the first week
- Gion Matsuri runs all July in Kyoto — main float procession is July 17, just after this itinerary ends; evening yoi-yama viewing on Jul 14–16 may be reachable if schedule allows
- Japanese-only restaurants, menus, and venues are no problem — Mom is a native speaker and kids are bilingual

## Nagoya Highlights

Nagoya has a strong food culture distinct from Tokyo or Kyoto:

- 矢場とん — the definitive miso katsu restaurant; multiple locations in Nagoya city
- 山本屋本店 — miso nikomi udon in a clay pot; essential for the udon lover
- 世界の山ちゃん — Nagoya-style spiced chicken wings (tebasaki), dinner only
- コメダ珈琲 — ogura toast morning set; free breakfast with any coffee before 11:00

## Details

- Train: Sunrise Seto (No. 7021) — Tokyo -> Takamatsu, disembark at Kyoto
- Departs Tokyo Station Track 9 at 21:50
- Arrives Kyoto 05:17
- Base fare covered by JR Pass; sleeper berth surcharge is extra

## Berths

| Type | Surcharge | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Nobi Nobi (open floor mat) | Free with JR Pass | Communal open berths, fine for teens |
| Solo | ~¥6,000-¥7,000/person | Private sliding-door cabin, 1 person |
| Sunrise Twin | ~¥7,000-¥8,000/cabin | Private cabin, 2 people (bunk beds) |
| Deluxe | ~¥13,000/person | Single cabin with private shower |

Suggested for family of 4: 1 Sunrise Twin (adults) + 2 Solo (one per teen), or 2 Solo + 2 Nobi Nobi.

## Booking

- Reserve at any JR Midori-no-madoguchi (green window) ticket office in Japan
- Reservations open 1 month before travel date — Sunrise berths sell out in days during summer
- Reference: sunriseexpresstickets.com for third-party booking options
- JR Pass holders pay only the berth surcharge; Nobi Nobi is completely free on the pass

## Notes

- No dining car — bring snacks and drinks from a convenience store before boarding
- Shower car is shared and requires a coin-operated token (purchase at vending machine near the car)
- Announcements are made at major stops; light sleepers should bring earplugs
- Arrive at Tokyo Station 20-30 min early to locate the platform and confirm berth cars

## Reservation

Traditional family-run Kyoto ryokan in the Nakagyo district, ~15 min walk from Nijo Castle. Wife's Japanese fluency will help significantly — staff are accommodating but the experience is deeply Japanese.

- Kaiseki dinner and Japanese breakfast included in room rate
- Private onsen baths available in higher room categories (request when booking)

Classic onsen town on the San'in coast, ~2.5 hours from Kyoto by Kounotori limited express (JR Pass covered). Seven public bathhouses (soto-yu) to walk between in yukata along the willow-lined canal — one of the best traditional onsen-town experiences in Japan for families.

Nishimuraya Honkan is one of Kinosaki's premier traditional ryokan. In-room private onsen available in select rooms. Kaiseki dinner and breakfast included.

Other options:
- Mikiya — slightly smaller, good value
- Kinosaki-no-Yado Mikuniya — quieter, good for families

## Reservation

- Not yet booked
- Book 1-2 months in advance for July

## Bathhouses (Soto-yu)

Guest card covers all 7 public onsen for the duration of your stay:

| Name | Notes |
|---|---|
| Sato no Yu | Largest, rooftop rotenburo, most family-friendly, separate male/female |
| Gosho no Yu | Largest indoor baths |
| Mandara-yu | Traditional and quieter |
| Ichino-yu | Historic building, cave-style bath |
| Jizo-yu, Kouno-yu, Kono-yu | Smaller and more intimate |

All bathhouses are separated male/female. No mixed bathing.

## Getting There

Kyoto -> Kinosaki Onsen: JR Kounotori limited express, ~2h 20m, no transfer.
Fully covered by JR Pass. Depart Kyoto Station platforms 2-4.
Trains run ~4x/day; check schedule in advance and reserve seats (free with JR Pass).

## Restaurants

Kaiseki dinner and breakfast included at Nishimuraya Honkan. San'in coast specialties:

- **Tajima beef** (但馬牛) — local Wagyu from Hyogo Prefecture; many ryokan and canal-side restaurants serve it
- **Fresh seafood** — grilled fish and seasonal dishes; July is off-season for the famous matsuba crab, but other fish are excellent
- **Canal-side cafes** — soft-serve ice cream and light snacks while walking in yukata

# Place: Hakone Open Air Museum (箱根彫刻の森美術館)

---
entryKind: place
category: Museum
propertyName: Hakone Open Air Museum
city: Hakone
StartDate: 2026-07-04
Duration: 2h
Cost: ¥1,600 adult / ¥800 child
OpeningHours: 09:00-17:00, open daily
---

Japan's first open-air museum, set across 7 hectares of landscaped gardens in the Hakone hills. The permanent collection includes over 120 sculptures by Rodin, Henry Moore, Picasso, Miro, and Japanese sculptors, displayed across meadows and terraced paths with mountain views.

Highlights include the **Picasso Pavilion** (indoor collection of 300+ Picasso works) and the **Symphonic Sculpture** — an 18m tall stained-glass tower you can climb inside.

## Tips

- Allow 1.5-2 hours to walk the full grounds comfortably
- The Symphonic Sculpture (シンフォニー彫刻) is a favourite with kids — the stained-glass interior is stunning
- Foot bath (足湯) on the grounds — free with museum entry, a nice break mid-walk
- Cafe and restaurant on-site for lunch; also a picnic lawn area
- 10 min from Hakone-Yumoto by local bus, or short taxi from the ryokan

## Getting There

1121 Ninotaira, Hakone, Ashigarashimo District / 神奈川県足柄下郡箱根町二ノ平1121
Hakone Tozan Railway -> 彫刻の森駅 (Chokoku-no-Mori Station), 2-min walk.

# Place: Senso-ji (浅草寺) — Tokyo

---
entryKind: place
category: Temple
propertyName: Senso-ji
city: Tokyo
StartDate: 2026-07-06
Duration: 1.5h
Cost: Free
OpeningHours: Grounds always open; main hall 06:00-17:00 (Apr-Sep)
---

Tokyo's oldest and most visited temple, founded in 628 AD. The main approach — 仲見世通り (Nakamise-dori) — is a 250m shopping lane lined with traditional sweets, fans, toys, and souvenirs leading from the iconic Kaminarimon Gate (雷門) to the main hall.

## Tips

- **Go before 08:00** — the morning light through the Kaminarimon is excellent, and the crowds are thin
- **Nakamise-dori** opens around 10:00; the lane is best for browsing on the way back out
- Omikuji (おみくじ) fortune slips are a fun ritual — the box at the main hall is shake-and-draw style
- Asakusa has excellent street food nearby: ningyo-yaki, age-manju (fried sweet bean buns), and melonpan
- **Sumida River** is 2 min walk east — morning river walk or short cruise to Odaiba is a nice add-on

## Getting There

2-3-1 Asakusa, Taito-ku, Tokyo / 東京都台東区浅草2-3-1
Tokyo Metro Ginza Line or Toei Asakusa Line -> 浅草駅 (Asakusa Station), 5-min walk.

# Place: teamLab Planets (チームラボプラネッツ) — Tokyo

---
entryKind: place
category: Museum
propertyName: teamLab Planets
city: Tokyo
StartDate: 2026-07-07
Duration: 1.5h
Cost: ¥3,200 adult / ¥2,000 child (advance tickets)
OpeningHours: 09:00-21:00 (last entry 20:00)
---

Immersive digital art museum in Toyosu where visitors walk barefoot through large-scale environments of light, sound, water, and reflection. Four main art works fill entire rooms — you wade through a shallow pool of water reflecting infinite lights, walk through a field of flowers that react to your movement, and enter spaces where the boundaries between floor, ceiling, and wall dissolve.

Designed to be experienced physically, not just viewed — excellent for teens and families.

## Critical: Book in advance

**Advance tickets are required** — walk-ins are not available in peak season. Book at teamlab.art well before the trip. July is peak summer and slots sell out weeks ahead.

## Tips

- Bring a dry bag or waterproof phone case — the water installation is ankle-deep and splashing happens
- Shorts or roll-up trousers recommended; you go barefoot from the entrance
- Allow 1-1.5 hours inside; there's no rushing but it's not a full-day venue
- The garden outside (チームラボ プラネッツのガーデン) is included — worth 20 min on the way out
- Lockers on-site for shoes and bags

## Getting There

6-1-16 Toyosu, Koto-ku, Tokyo / 東京都江東区豊洲6-1-16
Yurikamome Line -> 新豊洲駅 (Shin-Toyosu Station), 1-min walk.

Kyoto's most visited attraction — the head shrine of Inari, the god of rice, sake, and foxes. The famous 千本鳥居 (Senbon Torii) — thousands of vermillion torii gates — line a hiking trail that winds up Inari Mountain (233m).

The full hike to the summit and back takes about 2-2.5 hours. The lower section (to Yotsutsuji junction, about 30-40 min up) has the most photogenic gate corridors and views back over Kyoto.

## Tips

- **Go after 17:00** — crowds thin dramatically, the evening light turns the gates amber-red, and the mountain atmosphere is completely different
- The main torii corridor near the base is the most photographed and most crowded; it clears within 10 min of walking uphill
- Foxes (狐 / kitsune) are the messengers of Inari — look for fox statues throughout the shrine complex, often holding a key or jewel in their mouth
- Wear shoes you can walk in — the path is stone but uneven in places on the upper mountain
- Small rest stops (茶屋) partway up sell grilled mochi, soft-serve, and drinks

## Getting There

68 Fukakusa Yabuno-cho, Fushimi-ku, Kyoto / 京都市伏見区深草藪之内町68
JR Nara Line -> 稲荷駅 (Inari Station), 1-min walk — the shrine gate is visible from the platform.

# Place: Arashiyama (嵐山) — Kyoto

---

category: Park
propertyName: Arashiyama

StartDate: 2026-07-10
Duration: 3h
Cost: Free (bamboo grove); Tenryu-ji garden ¥500, main hall ¥300 extra
OpeningHours: Bamboo grove: always open. Tenryu-ji: 08:30-17:30 (last entry 17:00)
---

A mountain district at the western edge of Kyoto, combining a famous bamboo grove, a Zen garden at Tenryu-ji, a forested riverside walk, and traditional craft shops in a single compact area. Best visited very early morning when the bamboo grove is empty and cool.

**嵯峨野の竹林 (Sagano Bamboo Grove)** — a short path lined with towering bamboo that filters light into pale green; one of the most photographed scenes in Japan.

**天龍寺 (Tenryu-ji)** — UNESCO World Heritage Zen temple with an exceptional garden designed by the 14th-century garden master Muso Soseki.

## Tips

- **Go by 07:00** — the bamboo path is empty and quiet before 08:30; by 10:00 it's shoulder-to-shoulder
- Enter the bamboo grove from the Tenryu-ji north gate (not the Togetsukyo bridge end) to walk in the right direction with fewer crowds
- **Togetsukyo Bridge** (渡月橋) over the Oi River is the landmark photo spot — good in soft morning light
- Monkey Park (嵐山モンキーパーク) is a 20-min hike up the hill; worth it if teens want to see the monkeys close-up
- Rickshaw rides (人力車) are available; good for a short scenic loop

## Getting There

JR Sagano Line -> 嵯峨嵐山駅 (Saga-Arashiyama Station), 10-min walk to bamboo grove.
Or Hankyu Arashiyama Line -> 嵐山駅, 5-min walk to Togetsukyo.

# Place: Nara (奈良) — Day Trip from Kyoto

---
entryKind: place
category: Day Trip
propertyName: Nara (Todai-ji + Deer Park)
city: Nara
StartDate: 2026-07-11
Duration: 5h (day trip from Kyoto)
Cost: Todai-ji ¥600/adult ¥300/child; Deer park free
OpeningHours: Todai-ji: 07:30-17:30 (Apr-Oct). Deer park: always open
---

Japan's first permanent capital (710-784 AD). The main sightseeing area is compact and walkable from Kintetsu Nara Station.

**東大寺 (Todai-ji)** — one of the world's largest wooden structures, housing the 大仏 (Daibutsu) — a 15m bronze Buddha cast in 752. The scale is genuinely jaw-dropping.

**奈良公園 (Nara Deer Park)** — over 1,200 free-roaming sika deer (鹿) considered sacred messengers. They roam freely through the park, bow when offered shika senbei (鹿せんべい / deer crackers sold for ¥200/pack), and will absolutely poke their noses into bags looking for food.

## Tips

- Buy the deer crackers (鹿せんべい) from vendors near the park entrance — the deer will bow for them
- Deer can be pushy if they smell food — keep snacks sealed in bags
- **Kasuga Taisha** (春日大社) shrine is 15 min walk from Todai-ji through the deer park — worth adding if time allows; famous for hundreds of bronze lanterns
- July is hot — bring hats and cooling towels; the park has shade but the approach to Todai-ji is open ground
- The Daibutsu's left nostril is said to be the same size as the hole in its pillar — kids can squeeze through the pillar hole for good luck

## Getting There

From Kyoto: Kintetsu Kyoto Line -> Kintetsu Nara (近鉄奈良) Station, ~45 min, no JR Pass.
Or JR Yamatoji Line via Osaka (~70 min, JR Pass route but longer).
Kintetsu is faster and more convenient from central Kyoto.

Todai-ji: 406-1 Zoshicho, Nara / 奈良市雑司町406-1, 15-min walk from Kintetsu Nara Station.

# Place: Dotonbori (道頓堀) — Osaka

---
entryKind: place
category: Neighborhood
propertyName: Dotonbori
city: Osaka
StartDate: 2026-07-12
Duration: 2h
Cost: Free (entry)
OpeningHours: Always open; best 19:00-22:00
---

Osaka's most famous entertainment district — a canal-side strip of neon signs, giant mechanical crabs, food stalls, and restaurants running along the Dotonbori canal. Best experienced at night when the signs reflect off the water and the crowds are at full energy.

The iconic landmarks are the グリコの看板 (Glico Running Man sign) on the canal, and the かに道楽 giant mechanical crab (蟹道楽).

## Tips

- **Evening is essential** — by day it's a busy shopping street; by night it's one of the great urban spectacles in Japan
- Street food along the canal: takoyaki, okonomiyaki skewers, taiyaki, and giant grilled scallops
- The Glico Man sign is best photographed from the Ebisu-bashi bridge (戎橋) — stand on the bridge and look west
- Most of the best restaurants are within a 5-min walk of the canal

## Getting There

Osaka Metro Midosuji Line -> なんば駅 (Namba Station), Exit 14, 3-min walk to canal.

# Place: Shinsekai (新世界) — Osaka

---
entryKind: place
category: Neighborhood
propertyName: Shinsekai
city: Osaka
StartDate: 2026-07-13
Duration: 2h
Cost: Free (entry)
OpeningHours: Neighborhood always open; restaurants typically 11:00-21:00
---

A retro neighborhood built in 1912 to replicate the atmosphere of Paris and New York — now a wonderfully dated 1950s-atmosphere district known for 串カツ (kushikatsu), pachinko parlours, and the Tsutenkaku Tower. It has a rough-around-the-edges charm that feels completely different from modern Osaka.

The main draw is kushikatsu — meat, seafood, and vegetables on skewers, deep-fried in breadcrumbs, dipped in a communal sauce.

**Tsutenkaku Tower** (通天閣) is the neighborhood landmark — a 103m tower with an observation deck.

## The rule: 二度漬け禁止

Every kushikatsu restaurant has the same cardinal rule — **no double-dipping** (二度漬け禁止). Dip once, eat. If you want more sauce, use the provided cabbage leaf to spoon it. This rule is enforced, humorously but firmly.

## Tips

- 串カツだるま is the most famous spot — standing counter on Dobutsuenmae-suji
- Evening (17:00-20:00) is the best time — the neon signs come on and the neighborhood feels most alive
- Combine with a visit to Tennoji Zoo (天王寺動物園) or Tennoji Park nearby
- 15 min by Osaka Metro from Namba

## Getting There

Osaka Metro Midosuji Line -> 動物園前駅 (Dobutsuen-mae Station), 3-min walk.
