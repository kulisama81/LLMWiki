## Trip Overview

Two-week family trip to Japan for 2 adults and 2 teenagers (ages 15 and 17). Landing in Nagoya July 1 to spend time with family, then Hakone (onsen), Tokyo, Kyoto, Kinosaki, finishing in Osaka. Wife is Japanese; kids are bilingual Japanese, French, and English.

Route: Seattle -> Nagoya -> Hakone -> Tokyo -> [Sunrise Express overnight] -> Kyoto -> Kinosaki Onsen -> Kyoto -> Nara -> Osaka -> Nagoya

## 14-Day Itinerary

### Day 1 (Jul 1): Seattle -> Nagoya
Long-haul flight to Chubu Centrair. Rest and recover with family.

### Day 2 (Jul 2): Nagoya
Nagoya Castle (restored Honmaru Palace), Osu Shotengai (covered arcade, street food, retro gaming). Food: Yamamotoya Honten (miso nikomi udon), Yabaton (miso katsu), Sekai no Yamachan (tebasaki wings), Komeda Coffee (ogura toast morning set).

### Day 3 (Jul 3): Nagoya -> Hakone
Shinkansen Nagoya -> Odawara (~1h 45m, JR Pass), Hakone Tozan Railway. Check in to Hakone Kowakien Tenyu, evening private onsen.

### Day 4 (Jul 4): Hakone
Yunessun (swimwear-friendly hot spring pools/slides), Hakone Open Air Museum (outdoor sculpture park, Picasso pavilion), Owakudani (volcanic valley, black eggs). Amazake Chaya (historic tea house on the Tokaido trail).

### Day 5 (Jul 5): Hakone -> Tokyo
Odakyu Romance Car Hakone-Yumoto -> Shinjuku (~1h 30m). Explore Shinjuku or Harajuku.

### Day 6 (Jul 6): Tokyo
Senso-ji (early morning before crowds), Sumida River cruise, Akihabara (manga, anime, retro gaming). Ichiran Shibuya (solo-booth ramen), Gyukatsu Motomura (beef katsu).

### Day 7 (Jul 7): Tokyo -> Sunrise Express
teamLab Planets (advance tickets required), Shibuya/Harajuku afternoon. Board Sunrise Seto at Tokyo Station 21:50.

### Day 8 (Jul 8): Arrive Kyoto
Arrive Kyoto Station 05:17. Check in to Tawaraya Ryokan, rest. Evening: Fushimi Inari (best after 17:00).

### Day 9 (Jul 9): Kyoto -> Kinosaki Onsen
Kounotori limited express (~2.5h, JR Pass). Town walk, rent yukata, evening bathhouse crawl (7 public onsen). Overnight at Nishimuraya Honkan.

### Day 10 (Jul 10): Kinosaki -> Kyoto
Morning soak. Return to Kyoto. Arashiyama bamboo grove (go by 07:00 before heat). Omen Kyoto (dipping udon near Ginkaku-ji).

### Day 11 (Jul 11): Kyoto/Nara
Day trip to Nara: Todai-ji Great Buddha Hall, deer park. Kintetsu Nara Line ~45 min. Nishiki Market for lunch (street food, tofu, pickles).

### Day 12 (Jul 12): Kyoto -> Osaka
Tawaraya checkout. Transit to Osaka (~15 min). Dotonbori evening walk, Kuromon Ichiba Market. Mizuno (okonomiyaki since 1945).

### Day 13 (Jul 13): Osaka
Kaiyukan Aquarium (whale sharks), Osaka Castle, Shinsekai (kushikatsu). Mimiu (udon-suki hotpot), Daruma (kushikatsu in Shinsekai).

### Day 14 (Jul 14): Osaka -> Nagoya
Shinkansen ~50 min. Return to family, departure buffer.

## Key Travel Knowledge

### JR Pass Economics
- 14-day pass ~70,000 yen/person
- Child discount only applies under 12 (both teens pay adult rate)
- Covers: all Shinkansen, Sunrise Express base fare, Kounotori to Kinosaki
- Buy exchange order before leaving the US
- IMPORTANT: Only valid for foreign nationals on Temporary Visitor status. Japanese nationals cannot use the pass — calculate individual fares instead.

### Sunrise Express (Overnight Sleeper Train)
Tokyo -> Kyoto, departs 21:50, arrives 05:17.
- Nobi Nobi (open floor mat): FREE with JR Pass — communal open berths
- Solo cabin: ~6,000-7,000 yen/person — private sliding-door
- Sunrise Twin: ~7,000-8,000 yen/cabin — bunk beds, 2 people
- Deluxe: ~13,000 yen/person — private shower
- Reserve at JR Midori-no-madoguchi 1 month before travel date — sells out in days during summer
- No dining car — bring snacks from convenience store before boarding
- Shower car is coin-operated

### Tawaraya Ryokan
One of Japan's most celebrated ryokan, operating since the 8th century. Nakagyo district, Kyoto.
- Books out 3-6 months in advance
- Reservations via email inquiry; some categories only via travel agent
- Kaiseki dinner and Japanese breakfast included
- Private onsen baths in higher room categories

### July Heat Management
Plan outdoor sightseeing before 10:00 or after 17:00. Carry cooling towels and electrolytes. Rainy season (tsuyu) typically ends mid-July — expect rain/humidity in first week.

### Gion Matsuri
Runs all July in Kyoto. Main float procession July 17 (just after this itinerary). Evening yoi-yama viewing Jul 14-16 may be reachable.

### Nagoya Food Culture
Distinctive food culture separate from Tokyo/Kyoto:
- Yabaton: definitive miso katsu
- Yamamotoya Honten: miso nikomi udon in clay pot
- Sekai no Yamachan: Nagoya-style spiced chicken wings (tebasaki)
- Komeda Coffee: ogura toast morning set, free breakfast with coffee before 11:00
